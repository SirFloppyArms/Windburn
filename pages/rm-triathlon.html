The History of RM Triathlon

The Riding Mountain Triathlon has been a cornerstone of the Manitoba triathlon community for over a decade. Nestled within the stunning landscape of Riding Mountain National Park, this race combines breathtaking natural beauty with a challenging course that tests athletes of all levels.

Founded with a vision to inspire athletes and celebrate outdoor sport, RM Triathlon has grown into one of Canada's premier triathlon events. Each year, hundreds of competitors from across North America gather to experience the unique blend of competitive racing and natural wonder that makes this race truly special.

From its humble beginnings to becoming a nationally recognized event, RM Triathlon remains committed to fostering a welcoming, inclusive, and competitive community of multi-sport athletes.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does registration open?

Registration typically opens 3 months before race day. Early bird pricing is available for the first 100 registrants.

Can I change my distance after registering?

Yes, you can change your distance up to 2 weeks before the race for a $25 fee. After that, no changes are permitted.

Is there a team registration option?

Yes! Team registration (minimum 3 people) receives a 15% discount. Teams must have representatives from at least 2 different organizations.

What is the refund policy?

Full refunds are available up to 4 weeks before race day. After that, transfers to future races are permitted.

What time does the race start?

Sprint distance athletes start at 7:00 AM, Olympic at 8:00 AM, and Half-Ironman at 6:00 AM. Each wave has a 5-minute interval.

Is the swim in open water or a pool?

The swim takes place in Clear Lake within Riding Mountain National Park. It's a beautiful open-water swim with mild current.

What is the bike course like?

The bike course is primarily on paved roads with some rolling hills. The terrain is moderate to challenging with spectacular views.

Are there aid stations on the course?

Yes, there are aid stations every 5-8km on the bike and every 2km on the run. All stations provide water, sports drinks, and nutrition.

How can I volunteer?

We're always looking for volunteers! Visit our volunteer sign-up page at least 4 weeks before race day. Volunteers receive a free event t-shirt and meals.

What volunteer roles are available?

Positions include: Course marshals, aid station support, timing/registration, transition area support, and parking coordination.

Do I get any benefits for volunteering?

Yes! Volunteers receive free entry to the next year's race (same distance), a volunteer t-shirt, meals during the event, and volunteer hours for community service.

Can I volunteer if I'm also racing?

Absolutely! We have volunteer positions available before and after the races (before the earliest start or after the last finisher).

Why should my company sponsor RM Triathlon?

RM Triathlon reaches 500+ athletes and their families annually. Sponsorship provides direct brand exposure to active, engaged community members.

What sponsorship levels are available?

We offer Platinum ($5,000+), Gold ($2,500), Silver ($1,000), and Bronze ($500) sponsorship packages with varying benefits.

What does a sponsorship include?

Benefits include: Logo placement on race materials, social media promotion, booth/tent space during race day, branded merchandise opportunities, and networking with our community.

How do I become a sponsor?

Contact our race director at info@rmtriathlon.ca with your interest. We'll send sponsorship package details and discuss custom opportunities for your organization.

Course Maps & Resources

Explore each distance and learn the course before race day

Swim

The swim starts at the east end of the main beach area. Swimmers will proceed clockwise along a 750 m arrowhead-shaped course. Swimmers will pass the anchored sailboats as they exit the bay into the open water of the lake.

Lifeguards in boats as well as kayakers monitor the swimmers as they make their way around the course. Cheers from the enthusiastic crowd lining the beach carries across the water and encourages the athletes along the way.

The Olympic athletes complete a two-lap route, while the Sprint athletes complete one lap. Upon completion of the swim, athletes exit the water and run up the stone stairs past the cheering crowd and swim split timers, into the scenic transition zone.

Transition

The transition zone is a critical part of the race. Upon exiting the water, athletes run approximately 200m along a marked path to enter the transition area where their bikes and gear are positioned.

The transition is organized into clearly marked rows. Your bike number corresponds to specific rack locations. All racking is first-come, first-served (except elite seeding). The transition zone features proper spacing to prevent congestion and confusion during the race.

Athletes must exit the transition zone using the designated bike exit road. The path is clearly marked and monitored by volunteers. All bikes must be mounted before leaving the transition area.

Upon finishing the run, athletes re-enter the transition zone from a separate entrance, deposit their running gear, and proceed to the finish line area.

A detailed transition diagram is provided in your race kit. Familiarize yourself with your assigned rack location during race check-in.

Bike

Once exiting the transition zone, riders mount their bikes. A short cycle along Columbine Street takes athletes to the No. 10 Highway, where a left-hand turn, marshalled by volunteers and the local RCMP, directs the athletes north on an out and back course.

Considered one of the most scenic rides on the prairies, the cycling course cuts through the RMNP‚Äôs boreal forest. Riders are in awe of the mosaic of mountain and prairie landscapes, which is enhanced by frequent sightings of deer, moose, bears and the occasional lynx or wolf.

Both the Olympic and Sprint participants pass the famous Clear Lake Golf Course and embark on the first climb of the day. Once Sprint athletes reach 10 km, they turn around and head back to the town site.

The Olympic athletes continue on and are treated to a beautiful ride, capped by a panoramic view of Clear Lake just before they embark on a gentle 5 km climb to the turnaround. Upon return to the town site, riders prepare for their second transition as they glide past the enthusiastic spectators along Columbine Street.

Bike ‚Äì Profile

Understanding the elevation profile of the bike course will help you pace your effort and prepare for the climbing sections. The course begins at the transition zone (elevation 630m) and gradually ascends through the first 3km.

The most significant elevation gain occurs between km 8-15, where the road climbs steadily toward the park's ridge area. This is typically the toughest section of all bike distances. After km 15, the course descends and rolls through forested sections with variable gradient.

Super Sprint Profile: Minimal elevation change, ideal for beginners and those wanting a confidence-building ride.

Sprint Profile: Approximately 180m total elevation gain with manageable climbs. The final 2km returns to lower elevation with a slight downhill finish.

Olympic Profile: Approximately 450m total elevation gain with extended climbing sections. Two peaks are reached during the course, with corresponding descents providing recovery opportunities.

Weather conditions can significantly impact cycling comfort. Wind exposure on higher elevation sections may be noticeable. Check the course preview video and elevation charts in your race kit.

Run

Runners will exit the transition zone and head up Columbine and left on Ta-wa-pit for the first hill climb. For the next 2 km runners will pass by the seasonal residence cottagers cheering, keeping the focus off the hard bike and inspiring them on.

Please see the Duathlon Run Course map for the two run routes.

The Sprint runners make the turnaround at Deep Bay and head back to the finish line via Wasagaming Drive to the Lakeshore path. Olympic runners continue along the closed road for another 2 km towards the golf course before their turnaround. Shortly after the sprint turnaround runners head down to the Lakeshore path and are met by the view of the lake and some welcome shade to keep the engine cool.

With 1 km to go the trail climbs up and a vast view of Clear Lake and the sounds of the transition zone can be heard enticing them to speed up for the last push to the line. As the runners approach the end of this lakeshore path, they run into the finishing shoot inside the transition zone to the finish line where all the fun began a short time ago.

Run ‚Äì Super Sprint & Duathlon

The Super Sprint 2.5km run is an excellent introduction to triathlon. This distance is achievable for first-time athletes while still providing a meaningful running challenge after the swim and bike legs.

For duathlon participants (bike-run only), the 2.5km run concludes the race and serves as a final sprint to the finish. This course uses the same trail system as the triathlon distances but represents only a single loop on the primary trail.

The terrain is well-marked and relatively forgiving compared to the longer distances. One aid station is positioned at approximately the 1km mark. The final section features a gradual descent toward the finish line celebration area.

This distance is perfect for building confidence before attempting longer distances. Focus on maintaining steady effort and enjoying the experience. The scenic finish line provides excellent spectator viewing areas.

After finishing, proceed directly to the finish line area where you'll receive a finisher's medal, refreshment beverages, and access to the race festival with food vendors and celebration activities.

Parking

Adequate parking is available at the race venue with designated lots organized by distance and participant type. Athlete parking is located closest to the transition zone and race start area, ensuring convenient access to your bike and gear.

Spectator parking is available in separate lots with clear signage. A complimentary shuttle service operates between spectator parking and the main race festival area. Allow extra time for parking on race morning ‚Äì we recommend arriving at least 90 minutes before your wave start time.

Key Parking Information:

  • Athlete parking opens at 5:30 AM
  • Vehicle parking is limited ‚Äì carpooling strongly encouraged
  • Parking attendants provide direction at main entrances
  • Lost vehicle hotline available day-of (provided in race kit)
  • Accessible parking available (contact race director for reserved spot)

After the race, a coordinated exit flow helps manage parking lot congestion. Please be patient and allow extra time to retrieve your vehicle. Thank you for your patience!
