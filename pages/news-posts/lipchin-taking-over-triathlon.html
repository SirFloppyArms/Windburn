Lipchin Taking Over Triathlon

A paraphrased archive summary of the Brandon Sun story about the transition of Riding Mountain Triathlon leadership.

April 27, 2018 Source Summary: Brandon Sun reporting

The original article covered the transition of Riding Mountain Triathlon from longtime organizers Ellis and Deb Crowston to Dave Lipchen. The core message was that the event had reached a point where fresh leadership and a more professional growth path were needed.

The story emphasized Lipchen’s deep background in the sport: years as an athlete, international representation, leadership in Manitoba youth development, and his role with Windburn Multi-Sport Academy. Those details positioned him as someone capable of protecting the race’s legacy while pushing it into a stronger next phase.

The article also explained why the event matters so much. Riding Mountain Triathlon was described not just as a one-day race, but as a full weekend experience that athletes and families plan around. That event atmosphere, combined with the park setting, was one of the strongest reasons the handoff mattered.

Riding Mountain was presented as an event with enough momentum, tradition, and community support to justify a bigger long-term vision, provided the next organizer respected what had already been built.

The legacy coverage made clear that the outgoing organizers had rebuilt the race’s reputation over time, and that the expectation for the next era was not just to maintain standards, but to keep growing the event thoughtfully.
